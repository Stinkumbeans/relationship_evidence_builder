Why personal statements matter: Caseworkers read these first. A well-written statement explains the timeline evidence, addresses gaps, and shows the relationship is genuine — not just on paper. Write as though you're explaining to a judge who knows nothing about you. Be specific, consistent with your evidence, and avoid vague phrases like "we love each other."
Sponsor's Statement
Answer each section honestly and in your own words. Aim for 2–4 sentences per section. Caseworkers look for specific detail — dates, places, names — not generic declarations.
Applicant's Statement (person applying for visa)
The applicant's statement should be consistent with the sponsor's but written independently in their own voice. Matching word-for-word can raise suspicion — same facts, different phrasing.
Supporting Witness (optional but recommended)
A letter from someone who knows you both — a friend, family member, or colleague — confirming the relationship is genuine adds meaningful third-party corroboration.
Attach the actual signed witness letter separately with a reference code from the evidence index.
